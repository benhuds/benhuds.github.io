A Spotify Playlist With Your Top 100 Songs

Spotify always does a great job with their end-of-year summaries and “Your Top Songs of 20__" playlists – I love seeing how my taste in music has changed over time. At the end of 2019, Spotify presented a summary with my most frequent songs and artists for the decade overall, and I was hoping they’d wrap this up nicely into something like a “Your Top Songs” playlist as they’ve done for each individual year. I was excited to see they did indeed publish a “Best of the Decade For You” playlist. Unfortunately, I quickly found that it listed songs I definitely did not recognize.

As an alternative, I looked into ways to create a real “Best of the Decade” myself using the Spotify Web API. The playlist would contain the 100 most-played songs over the course of my Spotify membership. While I did find some useful documentation, I found two limitations that prevented me from creating the playlist I wanted:

  1. Spotify’s “Top Artists/Tracks” functionality doesn’t allow you to get your top artists/tracks based on play count. Instead, they use something named affinity, which Spotify describes is “a measure of the expected preference a user has for a particular track or artist. It is based on user behavior, including play history, but does not include actions made while in incognito mode.”

  2. Spotify’s “Top Artists/Tracks” functionality allows you to get your top artists/tracks based on affinity over a certain time frame by adding an optional parameter time_range. There are three possible values:
    • long_term (Spotify: “calculated from several years of data and including all new data as it becomes available”)
    • medium_term (Spotify: “approximately last 6 months”)
    • short_term (Spotify: “approximately last 4 weeks”)

For the purposes of my playlist, the only viable option was to set time_range=long_term, but the resulting playlist didn’t look like it included songs from my early days of Spotify (2011/2012-ish). Oh well.

Despite these two limitations, I ended up with a pretty good playlist and learned a bit about Spotify’s Web API. Check out the code on GitHub.
