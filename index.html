I'm currently a product manager at IBM, where I work on cloud data warehousing.

Before IBM, I completed my BA and MA in Computer Science at Wesleyan University, where I did research in programming languages and was advised by Dan Licata.

My research at Wesleyan focused on mechanized verification and semi-automated complexity analysis. I developed a system in Agda, a dependently typed language and proof assistant, to extract and formally reason about recurrence relations from functional programs. I've also worked on a wide range of projects outside of this, from theorem provers to data visualization.

I enjoy working on challenging, complex problems, which take me way out of my comfort zone. I also enjoy discussing them with others, and taking the time to learn new things whenever I'm not at work - lately I've dug into applied cryptography (hacking my way through the Matasano crypto challenges) and tech agriculture. You may have seen me at FOSS4G 2017, DSSS 2017, and OPLSS 2016.
