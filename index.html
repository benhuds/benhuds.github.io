I'm currently a product manager at IBM, where I work on data warehousing.

Before IBM, I completed my BA and MA in Computer Science at Wesleyan University, where I did research in programming languages and was advised by Dan Licata.

My research at Wesleyan focused on mechanized verification and semi-automated complexity analysis. I developed a system in Agda, a dependently typed language and proof assistant, to extract and formally reason about recurrence relations from functional programs.

I love working on challenging, complex problems, which take me way out of my comfort zone. I'm fortunate to be able to do this every day as a product manager. Outside of work, I like reading, racket sports, music, applied cryptography, information security, and the occasional side project. You may have seen me at FOSS4G 2017, DSSS 2017, and OPLSS 2016.
